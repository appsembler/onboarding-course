Developer

There are three types of developers:

  • Additional developers:
    • Short-term developers (temporary contractors), who have been hired for a specific task, scope of period of time. They are the most external members of the team.
    • Newcomers on probation (2 months, reconductible).
  • Core team member - the new recruits who have been confirmed become core team members.

Core team members differ from additional developers in that they tend to have more team-based responsibilities. For example:

  • Core team members are on a weekly rotation schedule, where they often have to take on some additional roles, including Sprint Firefighter, being on Discovery Duty, and occassionally leading our weekly sprint kick-off meeting.

However, in all other regards, all types of developers are put to the same expectations -- no politics or special treatment between short-term developers, newcomers, and core team members.

The only acceptable exception is when providing extra mentoring on tasks for newcomers (or anyone known to not have much context in the task), which is expected and useful.

Other Roles

Sprint firefighters, discovery duty, and epic owners (in the next section you'll learn more details on these roles). The "sprint firefighter" and "discovery duty" roles are on a rotation schedule, with different people assigned each week.

Who is responsible for answering emails from clients?

  • If it's an urgent problem, one of the current firefighters should reply directly and investigate.
  • If the email is addressed to a specific person, that person should reply (though they may reply and pass the thread on to someone else on the team, as described below).
  • If the client email is about a specific project/epic or we have a dedicated support epic for that client, the epic owner should answer them (or the epic reviewer if the epic owner is away, etc.).
  • If it's a non-urgent hosting issue, the owner of the current "Hosted Sites Maintenance" epic should reply, and schedule any resulting work for an upcoming sprint.
  • If it's a non-urgent, non-hosting issue, the owner of the "Small Projects" epic should reply, and schedule any resulting work for an upcoming sprint.
